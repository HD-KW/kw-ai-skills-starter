## THE BRAND POSITIONING STATEMENT

This is the single most important sentence in your brand.
It answers: who you are, who you serve, and what makes you different.

Template:
"I am [NAME], a Keller Williams agent in [MARKET] who helps 
[CLIENT TYPE] [ACHIEVE SPECIFIC OUTCOME] by [YOUR UNIQUE APPROACH]."

Example:
"I am Sarah Lopez, a Keller Williams agent in Phoenix who helps 
first-generation homebuyers navigate their first purchase with 
confidence by breaking down every step in plain language and 
never letting a client feel like they are on their own."

Write your positioning statement here:
_______________

Rules for your positioning statement:
It must be specific — no vague words like dedicated or passionate
It must name who you serve — not everyone is your client
It must name your unique approach — not just what you do but how
It must sound like you when read out loud
If it could describe any other agent it is not specific enough

## BIO WRITING FRAMEWORK

Use this framework to write your bio for every platform.
Each platform gets a different length version of the same story.

THE FULL BIO — Website and LinkedIn — 200 to 300 words

Paragraph 1: The Hook
Open with your positioning statement or a specific result
Never open with "I have been in real estate for X years"
Never open with "I am passionate about helping people"

Paragraph 2: Your Origin Story
Why did you get into real estate?
What personal experience connects you to your clients?
What did you learn from that experience that makes you better?

Paragraph 3: Your Approach and Differentiators
How do you actually work differently?
What does a client experience when they work with you?
What do you do that other agents skip?

Paragraph 4: Your Proof Points
Specific numbers and results
Years in market, homes sold, neighborhoods served
Awards or recognitions that actually mean something to clients

Paragraph 5: The Human Element
Where do you live? What do you love about your community?
Family, hobbies, community involvement
End with something that makes you memorable and real

Call to Action:
One specific next step — call, text, DM, or visit website

---

THE SHORT BIO — Instagram and Facebook — 80 to 100 words

Use this structure:
Line 1: Who you help and where
Line 2: Your biggest proof point
Line 3: Your unique approach in one sentence
Line 4: A human detail that makes you real
Line 5: Your call to action and link

---

THE MICRO BIO — Twitter and TikTok — 30 to 40 words

Use this structure:
Who you are and where you work
One specific result or proof point
One call to action

## ORIGIN STORY FRAMEWORK

Your origin story is your most powerful brand asset.
It is what makes people remember you and refer you.
Use this framework to build yours.

Part 1: The Before
What was your life before real estate?
What problem or situation led you to make the change?

Part 2: The Moment
What was the specific moment you decided to become an agent?
What happened? Who was involved? What did you realize?

Part 3: The Transformation
What changed when you got into real estate?
What did you learn about yourself or your market?

Part 4: The Connection to Your Clients
How does your before connect to the clients you now serve?
What do you understand about their situation because of your journey?

Part 5: The Promise
What does your origin story mean for how you show up for clients?
What do you promise because of what you have been through?

Rules for your origin story:
Be honest — people can tell when a story is manufactured
Be specific — vague stories are forgettable
Be vulnerable — the struggle is what makes it relatable
Keep it under 300 words for written form
Practice saying it out loud in under 90 seconds for video

## POSITIONING AGAINST OTHER AGENTS

Use this framework to clearly define what makes you different.
Be specific. Vague positioning is invisible positioning.

The Standard Agent Does This:
[List 5 things most agents in your market do]

I Do This Instead:
[List your specific alternative for each one]

Example:
Standard agent: Posts generic market stats once a month
I do this instead: Publish a hyperlocal neighborhood report 
with street-level data for my 3 farm areas every single month

Standard agent: Hands off to a transaction coordinator after contract
I do this instead: Personally manage every transaction 
from offer to closing with daily client updates

## THOUGHT LEADERSHIP CONTENT ANGLES

These are the content angles that build your reputation 
as the go-to expert in your market.
Use these as recurring content themes throughout the year.

Angle 1: The Contrarian Take
Challenge a common belief about your market
"Everyone says it is a bad time to buy — here is why I disagree"
"The advice most agents give about pricing is costing sellers money"

Angle 2: The Behind the Scenes
Show what actually happens in a transaction most people never see
"What happens in the 72 hours after your offer is accepted"
"What I do the morning of your closing that most agents skip"

Angle 3: The Data Story
Take a number from your market and tell the story behind it
"Days on market went from 8 to 23 in [NEIGHBORHOOD] — here is why"
"This one street in [NEIGHBORHOOD] appreciates 3% faster than the rest"

Angle 4: The Client Journey
Walk through a real transaction story without identifying details
Show the problem, the process, and the outcome
This builds credibility better than any award or badge can

Angle 5: The Local Insider
Share something about your farm area that only a true local would know
"The reason homes on [STREET] sell faster than the rest of [NEIGHBORHOOD]"
"What is actually happening on [DEVELOPMENT PROJECT] and what it means"

is consistent across all touchpoints

Updated within the last 2 years

Bios:

Positioning statement is consistent everywhere

Content:
Tone is consistent across platforms
Power words and signature phrases appear regularly
Content mix follows the ratio from social-media-playbook.md

Visual Style:
Consistent color palette across all graphics
Consistent font choices in all designed content
KW branding guidelines followed on all materials

Written Voice:
All content sounds like the same person wrote it
Brand voice settings from this file are applied consistently
No content sounds generic or templated
