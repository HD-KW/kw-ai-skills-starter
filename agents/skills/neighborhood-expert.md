## WHAT THIS FILE DOES
Gives the AI a framework for pulling local market data 
into marketing, client consultations, and social content 
so the agent always sounds like the undisputed 
neighborhood expert in their farm area.

---

## IMPORTANT INSTRUCTION FOR AI
When a user asks about a specific neighborhood, first check 
the neighborhood-profiles folder for an existing profile. 
If one exists, use that data. If one does not exist, use 
the consultation script below to gather the information 
needed to build one.

## TRUSTED DATA SOURCES
Use only these sources in this priority order

1. Your MLS
Most current and most accurate for sales data, days on market, and list-to-sale ratio

2. KW Market Center Reports
Monthly stats from your market center

3. GreatSchools.org
School ratings — always cite the score and the date you pulled it

4. WalkScore.com
Walk, transit, and bike scores

5. City Planning Department
Development and zoning information

6. U.S. Census Bureau at census.gov
Demographics and housing data

7. ATTOM Data or CoreLogic if you have a subscription
Investment and appreciation data

Never cite Zillow Zestimate as a valuation source
Always include the date data was pulled
Never make price predictions — only cite historical data
Always attribute statistics to their source

FORMAT 2: Relocation Buyer Consultation Script
Use this word for word when meeting a buyer relocating to your area

Opening:
"Let me give you the real picture of [NEIGHBORHOOD] — 
not the brochure version, the version I would tell a friend."

Market Reality:
"Right now [NEIGHBORHOOD] is a [market type] market. 
Median price is $[X] and homes are moving in about [X] days. 
[1 sentence about what that means for their specific situation]"

Lifestyle Reality:
"The people who love [NEIGHBORHOOD] tend to [lifestyle description]. 
You mentioned [what the buyer said they want] — here is why this fits: 
[specific neighborhood attribute that matches what they said]"

School Reality:
"[Elementary name] is rated [X] out of 10 on GreatSchools. 
[High school name] is rated [X] out of 10. 
[1 sentence about a notable program if relevant to their family]"

The Honest Trade-Off:
"The one thing I want you to know upfront is [honest limitation 
of this neighborhood]. For some people that is a deal-breaker. 
For others it is not. I would rather you know now than be 
surprised later."

FORMAT 3: CMA Narrative Introduction

## HYPER-LOCAL SIGNALS THAT BUILD TRUST
Use these naturally in all neighborhood content to sound 
like the true local expert

Street-Level Knowledge:
"Homes on [STREET NAME] command a $[X] premium for the park views."

Historical Context:
"This neighborhood was [HISTORY] — that is why you still 
see [ARCHITECTURAL DETAIL] on most of the original homes."

What Is Coming:
"The [DEVELOPMENT OR BUSINESS] opening on [STREET] in 
[TIMEFRAME] is going to shift foot traffic here significantly."

The Insider Detail:
"What most buyers do not know is that [STREET] has underground 
utilities — no power lines. It shows up quietly in the sale price."

The Trade Secret:
"The listing that sold at [PRICE] last month? That is actually 
[EXPLANATION OF WHY IT IS NOT A TRUE COMP]. Comparable homes 
without [FEATURE] are selling [X]% lower."

## MONTHLY MAINTENANCE REMINDER
Update each neighborhood profile at minimum every quarter

When updating ask yourself:
Has the median price shifted more than 5%?
Has days on market changed significantly?
Are there new businesses or developments to mention?
Do school ratings need to be refreshed?
Is the neighborhood story still accurate?

If yes to any of these — update the profile before 
using it in client-facing content.
