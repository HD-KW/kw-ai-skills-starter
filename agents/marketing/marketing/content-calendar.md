# Skill: Content Calendar
# Keller Williams | 12-Month Content Planning Framework

---

## WHAT THIS FILE DOES
Gives the AI a complete 12-month content framework built 
around the real estate calendar so a KW agent always knows 
what to post, when to post it, and why it matters that month.

---

## THE CORE CONTENT PILLARS
Every single piece of content fits into one of these 5 pillars.
Use these pillars to plan every week of the year.

Pillar 1: Market Expert
Content that proves you know your numbers
Examples: market updates, price trends, inventory reports,
days on market data, list-to-sale ratio breakdowns

Pillar 2: Process Educator
Content that demystifies the buying and selling process
Examples: how offers work, what inspections reveal,
closing cost breakdowns, mortgage explainers,
timeline walkthroughs

Pillar 3: Neighborhood Authority
Content that proves you know your farm area better than anyone
Examples: local business spotlights, community events,
neighborhood market snapshots, school updates,
new development news, walkability highlights

Pillar 4: Social Proof
Content that shows your results and client experiences
Examples: just listed, just sold, client testimonials,
before and after staging photos, closing day moments,
transaction milestones

Pillar 5: Personal Connection
Content that shows the human behind the agent
Examples: why you chose real estate, behind the scenes,
local favorites, community involvement,
your real estate philosophy, team introductions

---

## WEEKLY CONTENT RHYTHM
Follow this structure every single week of the year

Monday: Market Expert post
Start the week with data and insight
Position yourself as the numbers person in your market

Tuesday: Process Educator post
Teach something that removes fear or confusion
First time buyers and sellers share these constantly

Wednesday: Neighborhood Authority post
Hyperlocal content that only YOU can create
This is your unfair advantage over every other agent

Thursday: Social Proof post
Let your results speak — just listed, just sold, testimonial
This is your one promotional post of the week

Friday: Personal Connection post
End the week with something warm and human
This is what makes people remember you

Saturday: Stories and engagement only
No feed posts on Saturday
Focus on responding to comments and DMs
Reshare local business content to your stories

Sunday: Optional market preview or week ahead content
"Here is what to watch in [NEIGHBORHOOD] this week"
Keep it short and forward looking

## HOW TO BUILD A MONTHLY CONTENT PLAN

Step 1: Pull your market data for the month
Get current numbers from your MLS for each farm neighborhood
Update your neighborhood profiles in the neighborhood-profiles folder

Step 2: Identify your monthly theme from this file
Match the theme to what is actually happening in your market
Add your own local twist based on real community events

Step 3: Plan your weekly rhythm
Monday through Friday using the weekly content rhythm above
Map each day to a specific piece of content for the month

Step 4: Batch create your content
Set aside 2 to 3 hours once per week to create all content
Use the AI to draft each piece using the relevant skill file
Review and personalize before scheduling

Step 5: Schedule in advance
Use a scheduling tool to queue all content for the week
Recommended tools: Later, Buffer, or Meta Business Suite
Never schedule more than 2 weeks in advance
Markets change and your content should reflect that

## CONTENT PERFORMANCE TRACKING

Track these numbers every month to improve over time

Engagement Rate: likes plus comments plus shares divided by reach
Aim for 3% or higher on Instagram and Facebook

Reach Growth: how many new accounts saw your content this month
Aim for 10% growth month over month

Profile Visits: how many people visited your profile after seeing a post
High profile visits mean your content is creating curiosity

Link Clicks: how many people clicked your link in bio
This measures how well your content converts to action

DMs Received: how many people reached out directly from content
This is your most important metric — content that generates 
direct conversations generates business

Review these numbers on the first Monday of every month
Double down on content types that drive DMs
Cut or rework content types that get no response
