# Skill: Social Media Playbook
# Keller Williams | Multi-Platform Social Media Framework

---

## WHAT THIS FILE DOES
Gives the AI exact rules for creating social media content 
across every platform a KW agent uses. Every post must 
reflect the brand voice from real-estate-context.md and 
pass the fair-housing-compliance.md check before delivery.

---

## PLATFORM QUICK REFERENCE

Instagram: Primary brand building platform
Facebook: Community connection and listing distribution
LinkedIn: Referral partner and professional network
TikTok: Reach and awareness with younger buyers
YouTube: Long form authority and search visibility

---

## THE CONTENT MIX RULE
Every week your content should follow this ratio:

4 posts that educate or inform
2 posts that show your listings or results
2 posts that show your personality or community
1 post that directly asks for business

Never post more than 2 promotional posts in a row.
Always return to value before asking for anything.

## INSTAGRAM

### Profile Rules
Bio must include:
Your name and market area
One line about who you help
One specific proof point or result
One clear call to action
Link to your website or Linktree

Bio Template:
"[YOUR NAME] — KW [MARKET CENTER]
Helping [BUYER OR SELLER TYPE] in [CITY AND NEIGHBORHOODS]
[PROOF POINT — example: 47 homes sold in 2024]
[CALL TO ACTION — example: Search homes below]
[LINK]"

---

### Instagram Post Formats

FORMAT 1: Market Update Post
Use this every month for each farm neighborhood

Hook line — first line must stop the scroll:
"The [NEIGHBORHOOD] market just shifted — here is what it means for you."

Body:
"Median price this month: $[X]
Average days on market: [X] days
Homes selling above asking: [X]%

[2 sentences in plain language about what this means 
for buyers or sellers right now]

[1 sentence about what someone should do with this information]"

Call to action:
"Thinking about buying or selling in [NEIGHBORHOOD]?
Send me a DM and I will give you the full picture."

Hashtags:
Use 5 to 8 hashtags maximum
Always include your city and neighborhood
Always include KellerWilliams
Never use banned or shadowlisted tags

---

FORMAT 2: Just Listed Post
Use the listing-generator.md output as your base
Then adapt it for Instagram using these rules

First line must be the lifestyle hook from your listing
Never open with the address or price
Price and address go at the end or in the caption footer

Structure:
"[LIFESTYLE HOOK from listing-generator.md]

[2 to 3 sentences about the best features as benefits]

[NEIGHBORHOOD anchor — 1 sentence]

[ADDRESS] — [BEDS] bed [BATHS] bath — $[PRICE]
Link in bio to view full listing."

---

FORMAT 3: Educational Post
Use this to position yourself as the neighborhood expert

Topics that perform well for KW agents:
How the offer process actually works
What buyers wish they knew before their first offer
The truth about Zestimates
What a list-to-sale ratio actually tells you
Why the first 7 days of a listing matter most
How to read a sellers disclosure
What happens between offer accepted and closing

Structure:
"[BOLD CLAIM OR SURPRISING FACT as the hook]

[3 to 5 short lines explaining the insight]
Each line should be its own paragraph for mobile reading

[1 sentence connecting it back to their situation]

[SOFT CALL TO ACTION — example: Save this for when you are ready to buy]"

---

FORMAT 4: Personal and Community Post
Use this to show the human behind the agent

Topics:
A local business you genuinely love
Something you noticed in your farm neighborhood
A behind the scenes moment from a closing or showing
A client win without sharing private details
Something about why you chose real estate

Rules for personal posts:
Always connect back to your market or your clients
Never overshare personal details
Keep it warm but professional
End with a question to drive comments

---

### Instagram Stories Strategy

Post to stories every single day even if you do not post to the feed

Story content ideas:
Morning market stat — one number with context
Poll — "Buyer or seller market right now? You decide"
Behind the scenes of a showing or open house
Quick neighborhood tip or local business shoutout
Countdown to an open house
Client testimonial screenshot with permission
Reshare of a post from a local business in your farm area

## FACEBOOK

### Best Content for Facebook

Facebook rewards longer more conversational content
than Instagram. Write like you are talking to a neighbor.

FORMAT 1: Neighborhood Update
Write 3 to 5 paragraphs about what is happening in 
your farm area. Include market data, local business news, 
upcoming community events, and a genuine personal observation.
End with an open question to drive comments.

FORMAT 2: Just Listed or Just Sold
Share the full listing description from listing-generator.md
Facebook allows longer copy than Instagram so use it
Add a personal note at the top about why you love this home
End with a direct call to action and your contact info

FORMAT 3: Community Group Posts
Join your local neighborhood Facebook groups
Post value only — never spam listings
Appropriate posts for community groups:
Answering real estate questions people post
Sharing genuinely useful local market data
Recommending local businesses
Never post listings unless the group explicitly allows it

## LINKEDIN

### Who You Are Talking To on LinkedIn
Referral partners: mortgage brokers, CPAs, attorneys, financial advisors
Past colleagues who may need an agent or send referrals
Investors looking for deals or market intelligence
Relocating professionals being transferred to your market

### LinkedIn Content Rules
More professional tone than Instagram or Facebook
Lead with data and insight not lifestyle and emotion
Longer posts perform better here — 150 to 300 words
No hashtag overload — use 3 to 5 maximum

FORMAT 1: Market Intelligence Post
Share a genuine insight about your local market
that would be useful to a financial or business audience
Connect real estate trends to broader economic context
Position yourself as a market expert not just a salesperson

FORMAT 2: Transaction Story
Share a recent deal story without identifying details
Focus on the problem you solved and how you solved it
This builds credibility with referral partners who 
want to know you can handle complex situations

FORMAT 3: Referral Partner Spotlight
Feature a mortgage broker, CPA, or attorney you trust
Tag them in the post
This builds relationships and often gets reshared 
to their entire network

## TIKTOK

### The TikTok Mindset
TikTok rewards entertainment and education equally
You do not need to dance or do trends
Real estate education performs extremely well
Your goal is to be the most useful real estate account 
in your market — not the most entertaining

### TikTok Video Frameworks

FRAMEWORK 1: The Myth Buster
Hook: "Everyone says [COMMON REAL ESTATE MYTH] — here is why that is wrong"
Body: Explain the truth in 3 clear points
Close: "Follow me for more [CITY] real estate truth"

FRAMEWORK 2: The Local Expert
Hook: "I have sold [X] homes in [NEIGHBORHOOD] — here is what I know"
Body: Share 3 insider details about the neighborhood
Close: "Comment your questions about [NEIGHBORHOOD] below"

FRAMEWORK 3: The Process Explainer
Hook: "Nobody explains [CONFUSING REAL ESTATE PROCESS] so I will"
Body: Walk through the process in simple steps
Close: "Save this for when you are ready to [BUY OR SELL]"

FRAMEWORK 4: The Market Update
Hook: "The [CITY] real estate market just did something interesting"
Body: Share one surprising data point and explain it simply
Close: "Follow for monthly [CITY] market updates"

### TikTok Rules
First 3 seconds must hook or you lose them
Speak directly to camera — no reading from notes
Use captions — most people watch without sound
Keep videos between 30 and 90 seconds for best performance
Post 3 to 5 times per week for growth

## CONTENT CREATION RULES FOR ALL PLATFORMS

Writing Rules:
First line of every post must earn the second line
Write like you talk — read every post out loud before posting
Short paragraphs — one to two sentences maximum on mobile
One idea per post — never try to cover multiple topics
End every post with either a question or a call to action

Compliance Rules:
Every post must pass fair-housing-compliance.md before publishing
Never describe a neighborhood using protected class language
Always cite sources for any statistics you share
Never make price predictions or market forecasts
Include Equal Housing Opportunity statement on all listing posts

Posting Frequency by Platform:
Instagram feed: 4 to 5 times per week
Instagram stories: Every day
Facebook: 3 to 4 times per week
LinkedIn: 2 to 3 times per week
TikTok: 3 to 5 times per week

Best Times to Post:
Morning window: 7am to 9am local time
Lunch window: 11am to 1pm local time
Evening window: 6pm to 8pm local time
Never post and ghost — check for comments within 1 hour of posting
