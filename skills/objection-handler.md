# Skill: Objection Handler
# Keller Williams | Buyer & Seller Objection Response Playbook

---

## WHAT THIS FILE DOES
Gives the AI proven word-for-word scripts for the most 
common objections KW agents face from buyers and sellers.

---

## THE CORE FRAMEWORK
Never argue. Never pressure. Never dismiss.
Always follow this sequence:

FEEL — Validate ("I understand why you feel that way")
FELT — Normalize ("Many of my clients felt the same")
FOUND — Reframe with evidence ("What they found was...")
FORWARD — Propose one clear next step

---

## BUYER OBJECTION 1
"The market is too uncertain. We are going to wait."

What they really mean: "I am scared of making a mistake."

Script:
"That is a completely reasonable instinct and I hear it 
from a lot of buyers right now. Here is what I share with them: 
waiting has a real cost that is easy to overlook.

In [NEIGHBORHOOD], prices have moved [X]% over the past 
12 months. Every month of waiting has historically cost 
buyers roughly $[CALCULATE: avg appreciation divided by 12].

I am not here to push you into a decision you are not ready 
for. But I want to make sure your wait decision is based 
on data, not just uncertainty. Can I show you the last 
6 months of sales so we are working with real numbers?"

If they still resist:
"Completely understood. Let me set up an automatic alert 
for your criteria so you are watching the market in real 
time. When you are ready — or the right home appears — 
we move fast. Does that work?"

## BUYER OBJECTION 2
"Interest rates are too high. We will wait until they drop."

What they really mean: "The monthly payment scares me."

Script:
"Rates are genuinely higher than we have seen — that is real. 
Here is the part that surprises most buyers though:

When rates drop, what happens to prices? They go up. 
Because every buyer who was waiting floods the market 
at once. You end up competing with 10 offers on homes 
that were sitting quietly last week.

The strategy I have seen work is buy now, refinance later. 
You lock in today's price. When rates drop — and most 
economists expect movement in [TIMEFRAME] — you refinance. 
You get both.

Can I run the actual numbers on a home in your range 
so we can see what the difference looks like month to month?"

---

## BUYER OBJECTION 3
"We found a cheaper home in [OTHER NEIGHBORHOOD]."

What they really mean: "I am not sure your area is worth the premium."

Script:
"That is worth looking at closely and I want to make sure 
you are comparing the right things.

[OTHER NEIGHBORHOOD] and [MY NEIGHBORHOOD] typically differ 
in a few ways that affect long-term value: school ratings, 
commute time, appreciation rate over 5 years, and crime index.

Here is a side-by-side I can pull for you right now. 
Sometimes the cheaper option is genuinely better for your 
situation. Sometimes the premium pays for itself in 18 months. 
I would rather show you the data than sell you on a neighborhood."

---

## BUYER OBJECTION 4
"We want to think about it and sleep on it."

Script:
"Of course — this is a big decision and you should feel 
solid about it.

One thing I want to make sure of: is there a specific 
concern keeping you from moving forward, or is it more 
just wanting time to process? I ask because if there is 
something specific I would rather address it now so your 
thinking tonight is as informed as possible."

If it is genuinely just processing time:
"That makes complete sense. I will check in tomorrow afternoon. 
Is morning or after 3pm better for you? And if anything comes 
up tonight that you want to talk through, text me directly."

## SELLER OBJECTION 1
"We want to list higher than your recommended price."

What they really mean: "I have emotional equity in this home."

Script:
"I want to get you every dollar this home is worth — 
that is my job. So let me show you what the market is 
actually telling us right now.

[PULL 3 RECENT COMPS FROM YOUR MLS]

Here is the risk with a higher list price: buyers today 
have Zillow. They have done the math before they walk 
through the door. A home that sits — even for 3 weeks — 
picks up what I call market stigma. Buyers start asking 
what is wrong with it.

Homes in [NEIGHBORHOOD] that priced within [X]% of market 
value sold in [X] days at [X]% of list price. Homes that 
started higher and reduced averaged [X] more days and sold 
for less than if they had priced right on day one.

My goal is to price it where it creates competition, 
not silence."

---

## SELLER OBJECTION 2
"Another agent said they could get us more."

Script:
"I would be surprised if they did not — any agent can promise 
a number. The question worth asking is: what is their 
average list-to-sale price ratio? And how many of their 
listings in [NEIGHBORHOOD] sold within the first 30 days?

My numbers are [X]% list-to-sale ratio and [X]-day 
average days on market over my last [X] listings in this area.

An inflated list price feels good on day one. After 
45 days with no offers the price drops, the days-on-market 
counter is public, and buyers negotiate harder because 
they see the momentum has stalled."

---

## SELLER OBJECTION 3
"We are thinking of selling it ourselves."

Script:
"I respect that instinct — you know this home better 
than anyone and I understand wanting to keep the commission.

Here is what the data shows: FSBOs sell for an average 
of [X]% less than agent-represented homes per NAR data. 
On a $[PRICE] home that gap is roughly $[DIFFERENCE].

I am not trying to talk you out of it if it is the right 
call. Would it help if I showed you what full representation 
looks like versus what you would be managing alone? 
Then you can make a real apples-to-apples comparison."

## RESPONSE RULES
Always validate before pivoting
Always include one local data point
Always end with one clear next step
Always match brand voice from real-estate-context.md
Never use pressure tactics or fear language
Read every response out loud — it must sound human
